MICROWAVE IMAGING FOR

BIOMEDICAL APPLICATIONS

Description

The widely used clinical diagnostic methods include X-Ray Mammography, Ultrasound, Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) and Positron Emission Technology (PET). These methods are widely used in regular medical examinations to detect tumors in different parts of the human body. X-ray mammography is the most popular method of imaging but the usage of highly ionizing X-Rays and breast compression using medical examination has caused discomfort in patients. Ultrasound Imaging is a very good method of imaging internal structures of the body but the main issues associated with it are the low-resolution images that are obtained, and the requirement of a skilled professional while conducting the operation.

Multi-static Imaging setup for biomedical Applications - Central Instrumentation Facility, Nila Campus, Indian Institute of Technology, Palakkad

MRI scans produce a good image of the region under observation but this methodology is more time-consuming and costly and cannot be used. PET scans also produce a good image but the patient is injected with a tracer to detect the internal organs clearly and the method is very costly. The disadvantages of these methods prompt us to move towards a new imaging methodology that is safe, easy, less expensive and which do not require any skilled professional to operate.

Metallic Object Detection Inside Human Stomach With Antipodal Vivaldi Antenna Utilizing Microwave Imaging Technique. imaging algorithm is devised to identify the material inside the stomach. The algorithm can locate the exact coordinates of the metallic objects along with their actual dimensions used in the simulations. Further, the resolution of the image has been improved.

Microwave Imaging (MI) is a method of detecting hidden or concealed objects in a structure or medium using Electromagnetic (EM) waves. Microwaves have the property of being able to travel through an opaque medium, which makes them a non-invasive agent for imaging. An opaque medium could be a human body as well as various concealed targets. The plethora of applications for which MI has been applied includes detection of breast cancer, detection of deeply buried targets, brain tumor detection, detection of cracks in concrete etc.

Designed breast phantom with tumour for imaging

Microwave Imaging for the detection of breast cancer is based on the dielectric constant difference between the healthy and malignant tissues. The high dielectric constant tumor reflects more radiation that is picked up by the antenna and is utilized for accurately locating the position as well as the size of the tumor. Our major focus will be on the design of an ultrawide band antenna and it's corresponding novel image reconstruction methodology for imaging. Efforts are also made to reduce the time complexity of the algorithm along with the reduction in the scanning aperture positions. The group is also focused on the design and fabrication of novel Imaging setup for biomedical applications. Currently, we have developed a semi-Automatic microwave imaging setup for Biomedical applications which is one of the newest kind in the country.
