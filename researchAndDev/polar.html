METAMATERIAL ENABLED ELECTROMAGNETIC STRUCTURES

Polarisation Converters

Description

Metamaterials are artificially engineered sub-wavelength structure that shows unique electro-dynamic properties such as negative refraction, invisible cloaking, inverse Doppler effect, the reverse goos-hanchen effect and so on, which are not readily available in nature. The properties of these materials are due to their structure but not because of material composition. The periodic arrangement of these subwavelength structures (unit cell) in a plane is called metasurface. These unit cells arrangements are similar to the atoms or molecules which controls the properties of the electromagnetic wave in a medium. The architecture of the unit cell is playing a pivotal role in modifying the EM properties. Victor Veselago, a Russian physicist, proposed the concept of metamaterials theoretically in 1968. He predicted that there exists such a medium with negative electric permittivity (εr ) and magnetic permeability (μr) which gives rise to a left-handed orthogonal set of electric field vector (E), magnetic field vector (H) and wave propagation vector (k).

BROADBAND MICROWAVE POLARIZER FOR K AND KA-BAND SATELLITE COMMUNICATION APPLICATIONS

(a) Top view. (b) Side view. (c) 3D-view of the unit cell.
(a) Magnitudes and (b) phase of copol ryy and cross-pol rxy . (c) PCR (greater than 90% over 18.31–38.95 GHz).

A broadband reflective-type linear–cross polarization converter with high angular stability is demonstrated below. It exhibits PCR performance greater than 90% from 18.31 to 38.95 GHz for normal incidence. The simulation results illustrate that the proposed converter exhibits stable response (PCR greater than 90%) up to 43◦, and 45◦ for TE and TM, respectively. In measurements also, it exhibits angular stability up to 43◦ (for TE) and 45◦ (for TM) with considerable BW reduction compared with normal incidence.

Experimental Setup
Variation in phases of copol and cross-pol reflectances with different oblique angles under TE incidence.

Electromagnetic wave polarisation is the locus traced by the tip of the electric field vector in a plane perpendicular to the direction of propagation with time. Controlling and manipulating polarisation has essential applications like polarisation beam splitters, quarter-wave and half-wave plates, antenna design, and for RCS reduction in stealth technology.

Measured PCR at different angles under (a) TE and (b) TM incidences

The equivalent surface impedance model holds good with the simulation results for oblique incidence. The asymmetric width of the meander-line unit cell and its periodicity play a crucial role in shifting the destructive interference out to the operating band at higher oblique incidence. Due to the proposed prototype’s key features such as compactness, broadband, and wide angular stability, this design is suitable for satellite communication applications.
